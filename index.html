Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about NEXUS and how it can help you.

What technologies does NEXUS support?

NEXUS supports a wide range of technologies across frontend (HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Vue, etc.), backend (Node.js, Python, Java, .NET, etc.), databases (SQL, NoSQL), DevOps (Docker, Kubernetes, AWS), mobile development (React Native, Flutter), and data science (Python, R, ML frameworks). Our knowledge is constantly updated to include the latest technologies and best practices.

How does NEXUS personalize recommendations?

NEXUS personalizes recommendations based on several factors: your current skill level (which you can specify), the technologies you're already familiar with, your project requirements (performance needs, team size, budget), and your learning preferences (video tutorials, documentation, hands-on projects). The more you interact with NEXUS, the better it can tailor its advice.

Can NEXUS help with debugging specific code?

Yes! You can share your code snippets with NEXUS (either by pasting or uploading files) and it will analyze the code for errors, suggest improvements, explain potential issues, and provide corrected versions. NEXUS can help with syntax errors, logical bugs, performance issues, and security vulnerabilities across many programming languages.

How accurate are NEXUS's recommendations?

NEXUS's recommendations are based on industry best practices, current trends, and analysis of successful projects. While we strive for high accuracy, technology choices often depend on specific contexts. NEXUS will explain the reasoning behind recommendations so you can make informed decisions. We recommend cross-referencing with other sources for critical decisions.

Is there a mobile app for NEXUS?

Currently, NEXUS is available as a web application that works well on mobile browsers. We're developing native mobile apps for iOS and Android that will include additional features like offline access to your saved roadmaps and notifications for new learning content. The mobile apps are expected to launch later this year.
